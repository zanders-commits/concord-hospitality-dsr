Problem & Solution

One Platform for a Fragmented Stack

Great operators, fragmented tools. The data your teams need already exists — it's just never surfaced at the moment that matters: before the order is placed.

The Problem

Where Money Slips Through

  • −Punch-out platforms expose every item — GPO compliance is nearly impossible to enforce at scale.
  • −AP automation, forecasts, and purchasing live in separate tools. Disconnected data, surfaced too late at month-end.

The Answer

Ordering Intelligence

The $50 Miss That Costs $400

Orders placed at $950 when the Sysco bracket threshold is $1,000 — leaving $400 in savings on the table because the operator didn't know, and didn't think to check. They're not trying to lose money. They just don't see it until after the order is done and it's already too late. Reeco surfaces that information before checkout, not after.

Bracket-Size Notifications

When a cart is within a configured range of a Sysco or other distributor bracket threshold, Reeco flags it before checkout: "You're $50 from the next bracket — adding one more case would save you $400 on this order." The operator sees it while there's still time to act.

Configurable Approval Gates

If an operator tries to submit an order that misses a bracket, you can configure a gate: send it for approval, or block until the threshold is met. Toilet paper doesn't expire. A case more won't hurt. The system just needed to tell them.

AI-Suggested Additions

When a cart is close to a bracket threshold, Reeco can suggest items from that property's regular order guides to close the gap — relevant items that operator actually buys, not random fill. The right suggestion at the right moment, automatically.

All Suppliers, Side by Side

Because Reeco shows all suppliers in a single view — not separate punch-out tabs — an operator can see their Sysco, Guest Supply, and other vendor orders in one place. Moving items between suppliers to hit a bracket threshold is a visible option. Today, that comparison doesn't happen because no one has time to open three different systems.

FAQ

Common Questions

Can we keep using Avendra as our GPO while running Reeco?
Yes — Reeco is designed to work alongside Avendra, not against it. Your Avendra contracts, pricing, and approved item lists are loaded into the curated store we build for each property. Operators order through Reeco; your GPO relationship and rebates remain fully in place. We work directly with your embedded Avendra rep to make sure every item maps correctly from day one.
What happens to the Ottimate rollout we're 30 percent through?
Reeco's AP automation module covers everything Ottimate does — invoice ingestion, matching, GL coding, and Aptech integration — and because it lives in the same platform as purchasing, the match happens automatically. We would work with your team to map a transition that respects the rollout already in progress and gets properties fully live without disruption.
How long does implementation take for a portfolio the size of Concord's?
For a management company of Concord's scale, a phased rollout typically begins with a pilot group of 8–12 properties before scaling. Go-live on purchasing typically runs 4–6 weeks per phase; AP automation follows once purchasing is stable. Your Reeco customer success team manages the entire process — including working with your regional teams to build and validate the curated stores before launch.
Is Reeco SOC 2 Type II certified?
Yes. Reeco is SOC 2 Type II certified. Documentation is available for your CIO and IT team upon request. We would be glad to connect at HITEC or set up a separate technical review call with our IT leadership.
